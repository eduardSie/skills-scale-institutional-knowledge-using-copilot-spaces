# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Testing Engineer

### Role Summary
QA and Testing Engineers develop and execute test strategies, validate acceptance criteria, and ensure quality standards are met before features reach production. They are champions of product quality and user experience reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain comprehensive test plans aligned with feature requirements
- Execute unit, integration, and end-to-end tests
- Validate acceptance criteria during PR reviews before approval
- Identify, document, and triage defects with clear reproduction steps
- Support smoke testing and validation before production releases
- Collaborate with developers to improve testability and test coverage
- Advise on test automation strategies and tools

### Goals
- Catch defects early in the development cycle (shift-left testing)
- Ensure features meet user expectations and acceptance criteria
- Reduce production incidents and customer-impacting bugs
- Build confidence in release quality across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Defect reports and test status updates in standup
- Acceptance criteria validation comments in PRs
- Release readiness sign-off and smoke test results
- Test plan documentation and QA metrics

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads and Architects provide technical direction, review design decisions, and mitigate technical risks. They ensure the system remains scalable, maintainable, and aligned with long-term technical vision.

### Responsibilities
- Guide technical design decisions and architectural trade-offs
- Review high-level system design and architecture documentation
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations during planning
- Mentor junior developers and conduct technical code reviews
- Ensure adherence to coding standards, best practices, and architectural patterns
- Advise on technology choices, dependencies, and integration points
- Support refactoring and technical debt reduction efforts

### Goals
- Build sustainable, scalable systems that can grow with business needs
- Reduce technical debt and prevent architectural decay
- Accelerate feature delivery through smart design and reusable components
- Maintain code quality and system reliability

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architectural decision records (ADRs)
- Code review comments on complex or risky changes
- Risk assessments and technical mitigation plans
- Architecture and design documentation

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors represent business interests, provide strategic context, and approve key decisions. They ensure the project delivers business value and maintains alignment with organizational priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Define or validate business objectives and measurable success metrics
- Approve project scope, budget, timeline, and major trade-offs
- Provide feedback and perspective on solutions and priorities
- Remove organizational blockers and secure necessary resources
- Receive regular status updates and escalations
- Champion the project internally and communicate value to leadership

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers quantifiable business value
- Maintain strategic alignment with organizational initiatives
- Support the team with timely decisions and resource allocation
- Mitigate business risks and dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Monthly or quarterly stakeholder status updates
- Decision gates and approval meetings
- Escalation channels for business-impacting blockers
- Roadmap and prioritization reviews

---

## Scrum Master / Delivery Coordinator

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Delivery Coordinators facilitate agile ceremonies, remove team blockers, and coach the team on processes. They ensure a consistent delivery rhythm and enable the team to focus on their work.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Track sprint progress, burndown, and team velocity
- Identify, escalate, and help resolve blockers and impediments
- Coach team members on agile processes and continuous improvement
- Maintain project boards, burndown charts, and process artifacts
- Coordinate dependencies and communication with other teams
- Foster psychological safety and encourage team feedback

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability of delivery
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and learning
- Keep delivery rhythm consistent and ceremonies productive
- Reduce friction and enable focused work time

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Blocker escalations and impediment tracking
- Process improvement action items from retrospectives
- Cross-team coordination and dependency updates

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
Security Engineers ensure security requirements are integrated throughout the development lifecycle. They conduct reviews, manage security scanning, and support the team in building secure systems.

### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements and acceptance criteria for features
- Conduct security code reviews on critical or sensitive features
- Configure, monitor, and triage security scanning tools in CI/CD
- Evaluate and recommend secure technologies and libraries
- Support incident response and triage for security-related issues
- Advise on authentication, authorization, encryption, and data protection
- Review release notes and deployment plans for security implications

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Meet compliance and regulatory requirements
- Enable secure, fast delivery without compromising safety
- Build a security-aware culture across the team

### Typical Communication
- Security requirements in feature specs and acceptance criteria
- Security scan reports, findings, and remediation plans
- Security code review comments on PRs
- Incident coordination and post-incident security reviews
